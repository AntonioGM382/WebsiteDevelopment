Street Art

The term urban art or street art refers to all street art. Urban art encompasses both graffiti and various other forms of street art expression. Since the mid-1990s the term street art or, more specifically, Post-Graffiti has been used to describe the work of a heterogeneous group of artists who have developed a mode of artistic expression in the streets through the use of various techniques ( stencils, posters, stickers, murals, graffiti...), which move away from the famous graffiti but it is not always on walls because now it is possibly even expertly drawing in 3D.

One is the use of stencils, often with a political message, which became especially relevant in Paris in the second half of the 1960s. However, it was not until the mid-1990s, with the appearance of artists such as the American Shepard Fairey and his campaign "Obey" (Obey Giant) (Obey the giant), devised from the image of American wrestler Andre The Giant and carried out through the use of posters and templates, when the various proposals of this type they gain popularity in different parts of the world and are perceived as a whole as part of the same phenomenon or scene.
